The government responds to NR Narayan Murthy's criticism of its poll guarantee schemes, calling them "unconstitutional". The government claims that the schemes address the ideals of social justice and uplift Dalits, backward and oppressed classes, and accuses Murthy of making anti-constitutional remarks. The government also questions Murthy's silence on Prime Minister Narendra Modi's decision to waive loans of industrialists.


Congress defends the guarantee schemes, stating that they havel benefited 1.3 crore people and should be used for agricultural activities and the welfare of economically weaker sections. Kannada and culture minister Shivraj Tangadagi defends the schemes, stating that they are for the poor and not for the rich. Deputy chief minister DK Shivakumar acknowledges Murthy's suggestion for the development of people, but emphasizes the government's responsibility to protect the interests of farmers and the poor, especially in the face of rising petrol prices

"Murthy, who has raised questions about the schemes, appears to be against the ideals of social justice and has made anti-constitutional remarks,"

the minister said, adding that he should have also questioned Prime Minister Narendra Modi's decision to waive loans of industrialists.


Mahadevappa said Congress would have appreciated Murthy if he had said money should have been used for agricultural activities or the welfare of economically weaker sections of society. "About 1. 3 crore people have benefited from the guarantee schemes implemented under the leadership of chief minister Siddaramaiah," he said.


In Koppal, Kannada and culture minister Shivraj Tangadagi alleged that the "rich are unable to accept the guarantee schemes". "But we have not implemented these schemes for them. It is for the poor. We don't care about those whose stomachs are full," said Tangadagi, adding that the daily wage labourers and the working poor have "accepted the schemes with gratitude". However, deputy chief minister DK Shivakumar was more diplomatic in his reaction to Murthy's opinion, saying, "His suggestion was for the development of people. However, in the end, it is the government's decision. We must protect the interests of the farmers and the poor. How can people tackle the issue of price rise without assistance? Petrol rates have gone up to Rs 110 per litre from Rs 70. How do we tackle such issues?"



Murthy, during a chat at the Bengaluru Tech Summit, had said citizens who receive freebies and subsidies from the government should "pay back" to make the lives of their own children better. "Nothing should be given free, but there should be contribution from citizens also to make our society a better one," he had said.
